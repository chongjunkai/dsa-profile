Chapter 01

The spark

Curiosity became a way of seeing problems differently.

I have always liked finding out how things work. Technology made that curiosity feel powerful: a small idea could become a useful program, a working robot, or a solution to a difficult problem.

My first attempts did not always work. But every error gave me a clue. Slowly, I learnt to break big challenges into smaller steps, test one idea at a time, and keep going when the answer was not obvious.

“A mistake is not the end of the problem. It is the next piece of information.”

Web development

Science Workshop Website

I wondered whether science workshop content could feel more like an exploration than a set of notes. That question became SG Science Explorer, a website I designed and coded to make learning more engaging and easier to navigate.

The first version was only a beginning. Building it taught me to think about the person using the website, organise information clearly, debug patiently, and turn feedback into a better experience.

Chapter 05

The next chapter

This story is still being written.

I am looking for a school environment where I can deepen my computing knowledge, collaborate with motivated peers, and contribute through curiosity, effort, and teamwork. DSA is not the ending of this story. It is the opportunity to begin its next chapter.

Interview approach: answer with a specific example, explain what I learnt, and connect it to how I can contribute.

Why are you applying through DSA?

Computing is more than a subject I enjoy. It is how I explore ideas and challenge myself. DSA would let me learn in a community that shares that interest and contribute the experience I have gained from coding, robotics, and team competitions.

What are you most proud of?

I am proud not only of awards, but of becoming more persistent. Competitions and projects taught me that progress often comes after a failed attempt, careful debugging, and the willingness to ask for feedback.

How will you contribute to the school?

I hope to contribute ideas to computing or robotics projects, support teammates, and share what I learn. I also want to bring the discipline from piano and teamwork from football into school life.
